Scientific learning

Definitions:

  1. Scientific Learning:

    The acts of observing, testing, survey, project work and model building are known as scientific learning.

  2. Observation:

    The process of experiencing an object, event or process very carefully with the sense organs.

  3. Testing:

    The scientific investigation preformed inside or outside the laboratory to check whether the assumption is correct or not.

  4. Research work:

    It is defined as the in-depth study to find the answer of a question or a curiosity.

  5. Survey:

    The process of collecting data related to a topic or problem directly and deriving a conclusion.

Important points:

  1. Precautions to take in a lab:

    • Materials should be handled safely
    • Masks, gloves and goggles should be worn while handling dangerous chemicals.
    • We must wear an apron/ laboratory coat while working in a laboratory
    • Everyone should be able to locate the emergency exit
    • Hands must be washed after the experiment is complete
    • No eating or drinking in the laboratory
    • Chemicals should not me smelled or tasted
    • No playing/teasing in the lab
    • Must follow the instructions precisely
    • Special care should be taken when using the burner
    • Hot equipment should not be placed directly on the table

  2. Phases of research work:

    • Goal Setting
    • Making hypotheses
    • Choice of data collection method
    • Preparation of data collection method
    • Data collection
    • Analysis of data collection
    • Conclusion

  3. Steps for survey

    • Selection of objective
    • Choice of data collection method
    • Collection of data
    • Analysis of data collection
    • conclusion

Noticeable similarities (Research work and Survey work):

  1. Both have 'Choice of data collection' in their steps
  2. Both have 'Data collection' in their steps
  3. Both have 'Analysis of data collection'

Cell

Definitions:

  1. Cell:

    It is defined as the basic unit of life that is required for an organism to live.

  2. Cell wall/ membrane:

    Outermost layer of the cell that protect it from foreign objects.

    3. Cytoplasm:

    It is a vicious, sticky and clear semi-liquid that contains everything inside the cell.

    Mitochondria:

    It generates power for the cell.

  3. Plastid:

    They are colorful organelles within the cell. There are three types. They are:

    • Chloroplast:

      • It is the green-colored plastid

    • Chromoplast:

      • They are colorful plastids with every color except green

    • Leucoplast:

      • They are colorless/ white plastids

  4. Ribosome:

    They are the only organelles found without an outer membrane

  5. Endoplasmic Reticulum (ER):

    It is a highly folded membranous organelle making zigzag canals

  6. Golgi body/ apparatus:

    Bunch of bananas organelle

  7. Lysosome:

    Very small and rounded organelle with only one layered membrane

  8. Vacuole:

    It is the storage of the cell

  9. Nucleus:

    It Is the central part of the cell that controls and coordinates the function of every cell organelle. It has 4 parts. They are:

    • Nuclear membrane:

      It synthesizes proteins and enzymes

    • Nucleolus:

      It helps in cell division
