Scientific learning

Definitions:

  1. Scientific Learning:

    The acts of observing, testing, survey, project work and model building are known as scientific learning.

  2. Observation:

    The process of experiencing an object, event or process very carefully with the sense organs.

  3. Testing:

    The scientific investigation preformed inside or outside the laboratory to check whether the assumption is correct or not.

  4. Research work:

    It is defined as the in-depth study to find the answer of a question or a curiosity.

  5. Survey:

    The process of collecting data related to a topic or problem directly and deriving a conclusion.

Important points:

  1. Precautions to take in a lab:

    • Materials should be handled safely
    • Masks, gloves and goggles should be worn while handling dangerous chemicals.
    • We must wear an apron/ laboratory coat while working in a laboratory
    • Everyone should be able to locate the emergency exit
    • Hands must be washed after the experiment is complete
    • No eating or drinking in the laboratory
    • Chemicals should not me smelled or tasted
    • No playing/teasing in the lab
    • Must follow the instructions precisely
    • Special care should be taken when using the burner
    • Hot equipment should not be placed directly on the table

  2. Phases of research work:

    • Goal Setting
    • Making hypotheses
    • Choice of data collection method
    • Preparation of data collection method
    • Data collection
    • Analysis of data collection
    • Conclusion

  3. Steps for survey

    • Selection of objective
    • Choice of data collection method
    • Collection of data
    • Analysis of data collection
    • conclusion

  1. Both have 'Choice of data collection' in their steps

    Classification of living beings:

    Definitions

    1. Eukaryotic organisms:

      A eukaryotic organism is a living organism whose cells have a nucleus enclosed within a membrane

    2. Prokaryotic organisms:

      Prokaryotic organisms are unicellular organisms that lack a nucleus and membrane-bound organelles.

    3. Classification:

      It is the systematic arrangement of organisms or objects into groups based on shared characteristics, traits, or criteria

    4. Microorganisms:

      The organisms that are too small to see with the naked eye and are found everywhere around us in our environment.

    5. Virus:

      They are the smallest germs that can infect living organisms including other viruses

    6. Bacteria:

      They are the unicellular organisms that belong to the kingdom Monera

    7. Amoeba:

      It is a eukaryote found in water/moist soil/decaying organic substance/ body of host organisms.

    8. Fungi:

      They are unicellular/multicellular eukaryotic organisms, under the kingdom Mycota, usually found growing on dead/decaying substances

    9. Food preservation:

      Protecting food from infectious microorganisms and conserving the nutritional value of food is called food preservation

    10. Dry Preservation:

      It is the method of preservation in which the food is dried at a certain temprature with a certain moisture

    11. Wet Preservation:

      The method of preservation in which we dip the food in certain liquids to block the entry of organisms

    12. Cold Preservation:

      The method of preserving food by keeping them in low tempratures

      It refers to the variation in the same or different species

    4. Species diversity:

      It refers to the number and type of species in a certain environment

      The conservation of plants and animals

      The method which conserves animals in their natural habitat

Asexual reproduction

Definitions:

  1. Reproduction:

    Giving birth to own kind through any biological method by organisms is called reproduction

  2. Asexual reproduction:

    The reproduction through a specific organ without the fusion of gamets.

  3. Fission:

    The formation of new organism by splitting parent body

  4. Budding:

    A small bud appears on a body/cell which gradually grows into the complete organism and separates from the parent body

  5. Fragmentation:

    The method of growing fragments into a completely new plant in their natural habitat

  6. Regeneration:

    The growth of a cut/ seperated piece of the body into a whole new body

  7. Sporulation:

    Reproducion through spores

  8. Parthenogenesis:

    The development of a new body of organisms from unfertilized egg

  9. Vegetative propagation:

    The asexual reproduction through any one of the vegetative parts is called vegetative propagation

  10. Artificial vegetative propagation:

    Production of new plants asexually using tools and techniques for the improvement of quality and quantity of crops/ cashdrops.

  11. Cutting:

    The method of sowing & cut pieces of leaf/ stem/ root of some plants to get new saplings.

  12. Layer:

    The branch selected for layering

  13. Grafting:

    The process of joining 2 parts of closely related plants

  14. Stock:

    The bottom half in which the scion is tied

  15. Scion:

    The top part of the plant that was cut and tied to the stock

  16. Tissue culture:

    Preparing several plantlets under a controlled environment inside the lab from a freshly cuts small piece/ cell/ tissue of the plant, providing an artificially prepare mixure of plant nutrient is called tissue culture

Imporant points:

  1. Importance of reproduction:

    • It prevents extinction
    • It balances death rate by countering it
    • Its on the basis of the new generation
    • Balances the magnitude of biodiversity in the environment

  2. Types of asexual reproduction:

    • Fission
    • Budding
    • Fragmentation
    • Regeneration
    • Sporulation
    • Parthenogenesis

  3. Fission:

    • It only occurs in unicelluar organisms
    • After this process, the parent cell turns into 2 smaller cells, which then repeat the process
    • There are 2 types of fission:
      • Binary fission
      • Multiple fission
    1. Binary fission:
      • The parent cell splits into 2 halves
      • e.g. Amoeba and paramecium
    2. Multiple fission:
      • The parent cell splits into more than 2 little cells
      • e.g. Plasmodium and Algae

  4. Budding:

    • e.g. Yeast and hydra

  5. Fragmentation

    • Basically, the plant breaks and if its in soil it grows back
    • e.g. Ferns and lichens

  6. Regeneration:

    • If you cut it instead of leaving it dying, it will split into 2
    • e.g. Earthworm and Leech

  7. Sporulation

    • It will release spores into the air and if they land on soil a new plant will grow there, if the environmental conditions and suitable
    • e.g. Fern and Mushroom

  8. Vegetative propagation:

    • All parts of a plant (except flower) can be reproduced into a new plant.
    • There are natural as well as artifical methods to do this

      1. Natural methods:

        • Root:

          • Some plants have buds at their roots
          • If the roots get removed from the plant then, a new plant will grow there
          • Even if, the root is too deep to get sustainable amount of water, it will still grow as, the host plant will have plenty of food and nutrients stored
          • e.g. roses, sissoo, guava, etc

        • Underground stem:

          • They are of 5 types:
            • Sucker
            • Rhizome
            • Corm
            • Bulb
            • Tuber

          • Sucker:

            • Suckers, are roots made under the main stem that can grow into a plant (so basically fibrous roots that can grow into a plant)
            • e.g. mint, pisita, banana, bamboo, etc

          • Rhizome:

            • flat root with food and nutrients that lie just below the soil (basically a foot)
            • They are usually found in plants that grow every season
            • e.g. ginger, tumeric, bhojo and plants like ferns

          • Corm:

            • It has a few tiny buds growing out of it at the bottom of the shoot
            • e.g. Colocassia and gladious

          • Bulb:

            • It is also round and found under the soil, enriched in water and nutrients
            • e.g. onions, garlic, tulips, etc
            • It has depressions called "eyes"

  9. Signifigance of asexual reproduction:

  • It does not require male and female gamets
  • Male and female do not exist
  • It can be done by a specific reproductive part
  • They are genetically identical (except environmental effects)

  • Types of artificial vegetative propagation:

    • Cutting
    • Layering
    • Goatee
    • Grafting
    • Tissue culture
  • Advantages of artificial vegetative propagation
    • With this, only the best genes can be kept
    • Plants that grow faster will be obtained
    • Many efficient saplings can be grown in a short period of time
    • Plants that do not have seeds can be grown
